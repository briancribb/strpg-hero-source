Merchant Academy: 2 years (10/0)

Merchant academies spend two years on core skills and another two on specialty skills. The training isn't as advanced as Starfleet Academy training, but it's well-suited for civilian spacers. After the first two years, students are evaluated to see if they qualify for further training. A character who washes out may have to get a low-level position somewhere to continue training on-the-job.

Merchant Academy Command School

This optional school is required for any merchant who hopes to command a starship. The requirement to attend this school is waived for Starfleet officers who have completed Starfleet Command School or have two years of command experience in the field.

As with other certifications, the Master's Ticket is awarded after a series of evaluations and tests, so it's possible to learn the required skills on the job.

Engineering Specialty: 2 years (12/0)

General starship engineering. Merchant Academies are almost as advanced as Starfleet in this department, because you can't trade if your ship doesn't fly. After completing the program, students are licensed engineers anywhere the UPF/ITA is recognized.

Helm/Navigation Specialty: 2 years (9/0)

Helm and Navigation are combined positions for traders. People who take this specialty don't get the kind of deep, intensive training as Starfleet personnel, but they can competently crew either station. After the academy, Helm/Nav spacers sometimes specialize in one station or the other.

Financial Specialty: 2 years (11/0)

Where would the economy be without economists and accountants? This specialty is included because it was included in the old Star Trek RPG and because it adds flavor. If a player character takes this specialty, I'll add more economic and/or legal content to the story.

Characters who complete this program can choose to spend an extra point to be an officailly licensed Certified Public Accountant. Prerequisites for this perk are the Accountant professional skill and a familiarity with the law of whatever place the accountant chooses to practice.
