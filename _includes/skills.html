Systems Operation has been broken up into separate skills with a slightly lower cost. Some of them are also Everyman skills, but those only allow basic use. For example, every character can enter a set of coordinates into a transporter and activate it. However, they would need real skill to choose a location on their own or to deal with any problems which may arise.

Engineering Skills

Astronautics, Mechanics and Electronics are general skills in this game. Levels must be purchased for individual fields, but they apply for all three general skills. For example, if you buy a skill level in Sensor Systems Technology as a skill level, you can apply it to Electronics for repairing a bridge console, or for Mechanics if you're repairing a satellite dish. You could also use it for Astronautics if you're working on the ships sensor array.

Military Skills

Taking a skill level in Starfleet unarmed combat doesn't necessarily reveal a person as Starfleet personnel (although it could if the observer is an expert) but there's a martial arts package which is very distinctive. Choosing specific Starfleet maneuvers will broadcast to others where you were trained.

Everyman Skills

Skills aren't needed for things like Acting, Conversation or Concealment unless you want to be good at them. Even a familiarity indicates an above average proficiency.

This list assumes that the characters are in the Triangle, two years after the Four Years War with the Klingons. (Phasers are relatively new, so laser weapons are the most common weapon.) Players are free to take one, none or all of these skills.
