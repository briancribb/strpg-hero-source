Apprenticeship Basic Training: 18 months (7/0)

Apprentices work for their assigned sponsors to earn their keep and in return receive a program of instruction designed to supplement their work experience. After the first 18 months they're screened with a series of evaluation tests.

Especially promising individuals may obtain grants for advanced studies at a merchant academy, while the rest proceed to a second 18-month program of specialized training with one or more working professionals in their chosen field.

Engineering: 18 months (10/0)

Apprentice engineers are the same as academy engineers, because you can't go half-way on warp drive training. They're less experienced with general mechanics because their other skills are more commonly required for their jobs and 18 months can go by pretty fast. After completing the program, students are licensed engineers anywhere the UPF/ITA is recognized.

Helm/Navigation: 18 months (8/0)

Combat Piloting is a familiarity for apprentices, meaning that they can pilot starships under normal circumstances. Without access to the simulators that an academy can provide, it's difficult for apprentices to train for landing in storms or avoiding pirates.

Financial/Clerical: 18 months (8/0)

Characters who complete this program can choose to spend an extra point to be an officailly licensed Certified Public Accountant. Prerequisites for this perk are the Accountant professional skill and a familiarity with the law of whatever place the accountant chooses to practice.
